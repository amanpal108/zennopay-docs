A *corridor* is a (origin, destination, rail) triple. v1 covers two
destination corridors; origin is always a USD wallet held by the partner.

## TH — PromptPay via GLN

- **Corridor code:** `th_gln`
- **Local rail:** PromptPay (Bank of Thailand's instant payment network)
- **Provider:** GLN International (Korea-based aggregator)
- **Settlement currency to merchant:** THB
- **Refund window:** T+7 (provider-side)
- **Provider per-tx fee:** 1.0%

PromptPay QR codes are issued by Thai banks to merchants of every size, from
airport duty-free counters to street stalls. GLN handles the USD → THB FX and
the final push to the merchant's PromptPay-registered account.

## VN — VietQR via 9pay

- **Corridor code:** `vn_9pay`
- **Local rail:** VietQR (interbank QR standard governed by NAPAS)
- **Provider:** 9pay (Vietnam-based payment service provider)
- **Settlement currency to merchant:** VND
- **Refund window:** T+1 (provider-side)
- **Provider per-tx fee:** 0.66%

VietQR is the unified QR standard across Vietnamese banks. 9pay handles USD →
VND conversion and merchant settlement.

## Choosing a corridor

The corridor is locked when the payment intent is created and is embedded in
the session JWT. Your app picks the corridor based on which country the user
is paying in (typically inferred from the QR code itself; partner UX picks
the corridor *before* the user scans).
